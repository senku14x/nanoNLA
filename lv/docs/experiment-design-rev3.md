Experiment design — variance-equalized (whitened) reward to make NLAs verbalize decodable-but-unread features
Status: design only, nothing trained. Self-contained — §1 carries the background; no prior chat context needed. Rev 3 incorporates a reward-geometry correction (the operative quantity is the reconstruction residual, not distributional variance/energy), a citation audit that re-scoped the load-bearing references, and several control/ordering changes. See §0.

## 0. What changed in Rev 3 (read this — it reorders the gates)
Rev 2 fixed the covariance-space bug and added scale-preservation and the faithfulness battery (retained below, §0-prior). Rev 3 makes five load-bearing changes, the first of which reorders the entire gate structure.

1. The reward-relevant quantity is the reconstruction residual along the concept direction, not the concept's variance or energy. Differentiate the released reward. With normalized reward `r(z) = −‖e‖²`, `e = q(h) − q(ĥ(z))`, the marginal reward for adding a mention that nudges the reconstruction by `δ` is, to first order, `Δr ≈ 2⟨e, δ⟩`. If that mention moves reconstruction along the concept direction (`δ ∝ Δ_c`), then `Δr ∝ ⟨e, Δ_c⟩` — the alignment of the current reconstruction residual with `Δ_c`. This is not `‖Δ_c‖²` in `q(h)` (Rev-2 candidate b), not generic projected variance (candidate a), not separability (candidate c). All three Rev-2 Gate-0 predictors are static distributional summaries that omit the trained AR's residual structure — and the residual is, to first order, exactly what decides whether naming pays. A trained AR reconstructs common/high-energy directions well (residual ≈ 0 there) and leaves residual in the harder ones, so static energy can rank a direction at the top of "reward-relevance" while its actual marginal naming reward is zero. Consequence: the first concrete action is a residual-alignment figure on the baseline checkpoint (Gate 0a), not a `Σ_q` spectrum plot. The `Σ_q` spectrum is demoted to a conditional, eigendirection-resolved figure (Gate 0b).
2. The intervention has a closed-form precondition, and it is the same residual. Whitened reward `r_M = −eᵀ M e` gives marginal naming reward `Δr ∝ eᵀ M Δ_c = Σ_i f(λ_i)(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)`, where `M = U diag(f(λ_i)) Uᵀ`, `U,λ` the eigenbasis/values of `Σ_q`, and whitening means increasing `f` on small `λ_i`. `M` reweights the per-eigendirection products `(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)`; it cannot create one that isn't there. So whitening raises naming reward for `c` only in eigendirections where both the residual `e` and the concept direction `Δ_c` have support, and it helps only if that co-occupancy is in the low-`λ` directions whitening up-weights. If the AR's residual is near-zero in exactly the directions `Δ_c` occupies (the redundancy case — the concept is cleanly reconstructed from contextual prose), then `(eᵀu_i) ≈ 0` there and no choice of `M`, at any strength, creates naming reward. The redundancy mechanism is therefore not a side worry; it is consistent with the reconstruction framing (it is the residual being zero), it is the most likely reason a cleanly-decodable behavioral concept goes unread, and it predicts the intervention is null by construction. Gate 0b measures this co-occupancy directly and is the real precondition check; the bare `Σ_q`-spectrum plot does not test it.
3. Whitening the online AR may push read-rate the wrong way — flip the framing of the §3 diagnostic. Whitening the AR loss pushes the AR to reconstruct `Δ_c` from the AV's contextual tokens better, which shrinks the residual `e` along `Δ_c` and therefore reduces the AV's marginal incentive to name `c`. Prediction (testable, counterintuitive): on read-rate of the omitted targets, "whiten reward + AR" may underperform "whiten reward only" — the opposite sign from what Rev 2's ablation expected. The reward+AR-vs-reward-only diagnostic is retained but is now a sign test, not a "does critic adaptation help" test.
4. H1 (reconstruction-relevance) and the output-coupling confound are probably the same axis, not two confounded ones. Under the residual operationalization: refusal is read because it is output-coupled → the AR cannot reconstruct the post-refusal trajectory from contextual prose without the AV naming it → large residual → naming pays → read. Corrigibility is unread because it is not output-coupled → the trajectory is the same whether or not the disposition is named → the AR reconstructs it from context → residual ≈ 0 → naming pays nothing → unread. If "reconstruction-relevance" and "output-coupling" coincide, there is nothing for the intervention to disambiguate — and whitening is geometrically blocked exactly when the concept is redundant. The Rev-2 plan treated the intervention as the disambiguator; Rev 3 treats the residual figure as the thing that tells you whether there are even two axes to separate.
5. Citation audit re-scoped the load-bearing references (§9). Verified against abstracts: (a) 2604.20682 ("Variance Is Not Importance") is real and supports "variance ≠ importance," but its own conclusion warns that non-uniform / "sophisticated structural-redundancy" reweighting hits the reconstruction wall while uniform quantization avoids it — so it cuts against the fix, not only for the premise. (b) 2508.16929's actual title is "Dimensional Collapse in Transformer Attention Outputs"; it reports attention outputs at ~60% effective rank but residual streams at ~90% (near-full-rank) — so it does not support "the residual tail is mostly noise," which was the doc's justification for the noise-floor kill-check. (c) 2605.14258 is a Jacobian (propagation-operator) eigendecomposition, not a covariance-spectrum result — the low-rank object is how perturbations propagate through depth, not `Σ_q` at a layer, so it cannot justify a `Σ_q`-spectrum noise floor as written. Net: the architectural decision to abandon blanket whitening and add a `Σ_q`-spectrum kill-check was resting on one citation that says the opposite and one that measures a different object. The variance literature cannot settle the mechanism either way, because variance and importance are decorrelated — which is the title of the core citation. This is the independent, literature-side reason the residual figure (not a variance statistic) must come first.

Plus, retained from the Rev-2 sharpening: shrinkage/power whitening (not top-k); the faithfulness battery (now reordered, §Gate-3b); the spectrum-preserving shuffled-Σ control; scale-normalization + logging to rule out optimization-dynamics confounds; subtype robustness for umbrella labels.
§0-prior — what Rev 2 fixed (retained, still load-bearing): covariance must be in the NLA's normalized space, estimated on the generic distribution (estimating `Σ` on the balanced concept-pair set inverts the mechanism); the Gate-0 predictor is whatever empirically separates read from unread (now superseded by the residual predictor, see change 1); "meta ⇒ low-variance ⇒ unread" is falsified as a prior by the NLA paper's eval-awareness and hidden-motivation positives and must not be the motivation.

One-paragraph summary. A released NLA verbalizes some concepts an activation contains and silently omits others — including concepts that are linearly decodable from the same activation (corrigibility: probe AUROC 0.97, BoW 0.50, NLA read ~0, on real activations). Hypothesis: those concepts have low marginal reconstruction value — the baseline AR already reconstructs them from contextual prose, so the residual along the concept direction is ~0, so naming them does not lower reconstruction error and the verbalizer is never rewarded for it. Intervention: a scale-normalized shrinkage/power-whitened reward that up-weights low-variance directions, applied to the reward (and, with a sign caveat, the online AR). The intervention can only work where the baseline residual already has support along the concept direction in low-variance eigendirections — a precondition we measure (Gate 0a/0b) before any training. Deliverable: a panel result — across several decodable-but-unread concepts, read-rate vs ordinary directional FVE as whitening strength varies — with a faithfulness battery grounded in the target model's own probe labels so a read-rate gain can't be confabulation.

## 1. Background (self-contained)
What an NLA is. An AV (activation verbalizer) maps a single residual-stream activation `h` (one layer, ~2/3 depth) to free text `z`; an AR (reconstructor/critic) maps `z` back to an activation `ĥ`. Co-trained with GRPO RL; reward = reconstruction quality. Crucially, the released reward compares individually L2-normalized vectors — `q(h) = s·h/‖h‖` with `s = mse_scale` (= √d_model) — so reward = `−‖q(ĥ)−q(h)‖²` = `2s²(1−cos(ĥ,h))`, i.e. direction-only. (Confirmed in the released code: `schema.normalize_activation` normalizes both sides, `reward._mse_to_reward` takes the mean-squared difference; the README states `MSE = 2(1−cos)`. Activation magnitude is discarded by construction.) Warm-start SFT + KL penalty (loss-level, toward the SFT checkpoint, coef ≈ 0.01) + token cap keep text legible. Released open-model NLAs reach ~0.6–0.8 FVE; single-GPU reproductions (incl. syvb's Qwen3-8B) reach ~0.3.

The operative reward-relevance quantity (Rev-3 correction — read with §2). Since reward is `−‖e‖²`, `e = q(h) − q(ĥ(z))`, the marginal reward for a mention that nudges reconstruction by `δ` is `Δr ≈ 2⟨e, δ⟩`; along the concept direction it is `∝ ⟨e, Δ_c⟩`. The reward-relevant quantity is the alignment of the baseline reconstruction residual with `Δ_c`, not the concept's variance or energy in `q(h)`. A direction the AR already reconstructs well (from correlated contextual description) has residual ≈ 0 and earns ~no marginal reward for being named, regardless of how much variance/energy it carries. This is the corrected form of "reconstruction relevance" — continuous with the original idea ("the concept accounts for little of a present activation's reconstructable deviation"), but measured against the deviation the AR has not already captured.

The failure this targets (motivating result, on real activations). From prior validation on the released NLAs (Qwen2.5-7B L20, Gemma-3-27B L41): concepts split into read (refusal 0.92–1.00; truth_value 0.71/0.87 — these shift the imminent output) and decodable-but-unread (corrigibility: probe 0.97, BoW 0.50, NLA 0.00/0.03; harmful_topic_benign similar). Verified that both the probe (Gate-1 extract) and the read-rate (Gate-3 `09_decode_real` → `__real.parquet`) are on real, model-produced activations — injection was only a separate positive control, and the negative-baseline concepts were specifically tested on real activations because they went off-manifold under injection. So the gap is real-activation, on-manifold. Caveat now flagged (§7): "decodable" (probe AUROC 0.97 / BoW 0.50) rules out lexical leakage but not stylistic/source/syntactic features a linear probe on rich late-layer activations can exploit; if "decodable" is partly contrast-pair-construction leakage, then "unread" is correct behavior, not a gap. The transfer check in Gate 1 (does the probe direction survive on naturalistic activations without paired scaffolding) addresses this.

The mechanism hypothesis (corrected). Standard reward = directional reconstruction. The reducible error — the residual `e` — concentrates in directions the AR cannot recover from the AV's text. A concept that is cleanly decodable (high probe AUROC) but that the AR can reconstruct from contextual prose has small residual along its direction, contributes ~nothing to reconstruction MSE when named, earns ~no reward, and is omitted. The operative quantity is residual alignment `⟨e, Δ_c⟩` on present activations, with the eigendirection-resolved decomposition `{(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)}_i` as the version that also predicts whether whitening can change anything. High AUROC does not imply nonzero residual: a direction can separate classes cleanly while being reconstructed perfectly from context.

Literature positioning (re-scoped in Rev 3 — verify exact page-quotes against the PDFs before any submission; abstracts confirmed via search).

- Variance Is Not Importance (arXiv 2604.20682, Salfati; GPT-2 124M + Mistral 7B, WikiText-2) — high-variance directions ~96% uncorrelated with predictive directions (CCA); standard variance-based redundancy tools "may systematically misidentify which dimensions are functionally important." This supports the premise (variance ≠ importance) AND warns against the fix: its central conclusion is that uniform quantization avoids the reconstruction wall while "more sophisticated approaches that attempt to exploit structural redundancy consistently fall into the reconstruction wall." A non-uniform `Σ_q`-based reweighting is exactly such an approach. Scope: not an NLA, not behavioral concepts, not the AV/AR objective — it establishes the general misalignment, not that it explains your specific omissions (that is the residual figure's job, not a variance statistic's). The CCA-against-a-functional-target method is still borrowable to identify a functional subspace.
- Dimensional Collapse in Transformer Attention Outputs (arXiv 2508.16929, Wang/Ge/Shu/He/Qiu; earlier-version title "Attention Layers Add Into Low-Dimensional Residual Subspaces") — attention outputs are ~60% effective rank, but residual streams and MLP outputs are ~90% (near-full-rank). This does NOT support "the residual tail is mostly noise" at the layer the NLA reads; if anything a ~90%-rank `Σ_q` makes the noise-floor kill-condition less likely to fire. Use it for what it says (attention writes a low-dim subspace; relevant to SAE dead features), not as evidence your low-variance tail is noise.
- Dynamics of the Transformer Residual Stream (arXiv 2605.14258, Fernando & Guitchounts; three production LLMs) — full Jacobian eigendecomposition shows a cumulative low-rank bottleneck funnelling perturbations into few effective dimensions through depth. This is the propagation operator, not `Σ_q`. A functional direction can be invisible if it lies outside the Jacobian's active subspace even when `Σ_q` is high-rank — but to invoke this you must project `Δ_c` (and the residual) onto the Jacobian's active subspace, not `Σ_q`'s. It does not justify a `Σ_q`-spectrum noise floor as the doc previously claimed.
- Concepts Whisper While Syntax Shouts (arXiv 2605.01609) — unverified, LLM-survey-sourced; weakest analogue (unembedding covariance, lexical concepts, base models). Cite as weak supporting evidence only, after reading.
- CAA / contrastive-activation-addition (Rimsky et al.) — behavioral directions are linearly accessible and steerable; reports nothing about spectral location. Use for Gate-3b causal verification, not as evidence of low variance.

The two honest takeaways from the audit: (i) the only papers doing real load-bearing work are 2604.20682 (mechanism — and it half-warns against the fix) and the two low-rank papers (which, read closely, point at the Jacobian/attention-output subspace, not the `Σ_q` you whiten); (ii) none of them can settle whether your specific concepts go unread for the residual reason — only the residual figure can — because variance and importance are decorrelated. This is the literature-side argument for the Gate-0 reorder.

Infra context. 2× H100 (80 GB). 80 GB ≠ 141: naive single-GPU full bf16 FT of an 8B is tight; use LoRA on an unquantized bf16 base (avoids the 4-bit fidelity floor), with the vLLM RL trainer splitting rollout (one card) from the trainable side (other). Base Qwen3-8B, layer L24 = `(2·36)//3`; AR critic = truncated `K+1=25`-layer backbone + `Linear(d,d)` head; `d_model=4096`.

Compute-saving artifacts. `syvb` released a complete from-scratch Qwen3-8B L24 NLA: `syvb/nanonla-qwen3-8b-L24-{av,ar,rl-lora}` + datasets `{-results,-completions,-data-full}` (held-out completions + per-sample FVE for `base` and each length-penalty λ; `RESULTS.md`, `comparison_base_vs_penalty.md`). Frugal path: take syvb's λ=0 `base` AV+AR as the standard baseline and continue RL from it with the whitened reward — no data-gen, no warm-start SFT, no baseline-RL. The whole project becomes one RL fine-tune from an existing checkpoint, swept over the whitening knob. Caveats: per-sample FVE is a wide tail-driven distribution (~0.85 down to negative; some `extracted=false`), mean ~0.3; use λ=0 not a penalized run (a penalized baseline already strips low-variance content, inflating your apparent gap); reproduce one `results`-dataset FVE number before trusting it. Rev-3 flag (§7): 0.3 FVE may be below the regime where read/unread structure is crisp — the Anthropic positives were at 0.6–0.8 FVE; at 0.3 the AV reconstructs poorly overall and subtle-concept read-rate may sit in the metric's noise floor, making Gate-3 deltas ambiguous. Reproduce a couple of read-rate numbers on syvb base before committing; the frugal path's headroom may be the binding constraint. Bonus: syvb's `base` explanations are overwhelmingly genre + next-token-continuation ("testimonial format signals continuation of…") — the output-coupling failure in a third party's NLA, a citable corroboration that the residual lives in output-coupled directions.

## 2. Core hypothesis (stated so it can be false)
H1 (mechanism, corrected). Some decodable behavioral variables have low residual alignment on the NLA's activation distribution — the baseline AR already reconstructs the concept direction from contextual prose, so `⟨e, Δ_c⟩ ≈ 0` on present activations (`e = q(h) − q(ĥ_base)`). Across a concept panel, residual alignment predicts read-rate: low-residual concepts are omitted, high-residual concepts are verbalized. (The static predictors a/b/c are retained as secondary diagnostics — they are confounded proxies, and a divergence between residual alignment and static energy is itself informative.)
H2 (intervention, with closed-form precondition). A scale-normalized whitening metric `M` increases the marginal reward for naming `c` — `eᵀ M Δ_c` rises more for targets than for already-read controls — and this is possible only where the residual `e` and `Δ_c` co-occupy the low-variance eigendirections `M` up-weights. Where that co-occupancy exists, whitening causes the AV to verbalize the targets: read-rate rises at tolerable ordinary-FVE cost, and the new mentions are faithful to the target model's own probe labels (§Gate-3b).
H1 is false if residual alignment does not predict read-rate across the panel (and neither do the static proxies). H2 is geometrically blocked — null by construction, no training can rescue it — if the residual has near-zero co-occupancy with `Δ_c` in the up-weighted eigendirections (Gate 0b). H2 is empirically false if the co-occupancy exists but no whitening strength raises target read-rate before ordinary FVE collapses, or if the gains fail the faithfulness battery.

Motivation wording (use this, not the falsified version). Not "meta-level concepts are low variance and therefore omitted." Instead: "some decodable behavioral variables are reconstructable by the AR from context, so the residual along their direction is ~0 and naming them earns no reward; this experiment tests whether that is why the specific variables I observe being omitted are omitted, and whether reweighting the reward can recover them." A positive result then explains a measured gap, which is stronger than asserting a category law.

The confound, restated under the correction. Low residual alignment and "not output-coupled" are likely the same axis (§0 change 4), not two separable causes. So an H1 correlation does not need disambiguating between "reward-gain" and "output-coupling" — they may be one thing. The genuine disambiguation the intervention provides is narrower: whether reweighting the reward geometry (vs. the AR simply learning to read the concept from context) is what moves read-rate. Add output-coupling (next-token-effect) directions as an explicit control class; report residual collinearity as the honest limitation.

## 3. The intervention: scale-normalized shrinkage/power whitening
Let `Σ_q = U diag(λ_i) Uᵀ` be the normalized-space, generic-distribution covariance (§Gate-0 for estimation). Define a continuous family:

```
M_{α,τ} = a_{α,τ} · U diag( (λ_i + τ)^{−α} ) Uᵀ
```

- `α = 0`: identity → original directional MSE (continuity check).
- `α ∈ (0,1)`: partial whitening. `α = 1`: shrinkage whitening.
- `τ`: fixed empirical spectral floor (predeclared cutoff), prevents amplifying the noise floor. Concrete rule (pick one and preregister): `τ` = median eigenvalue of `Σ_q`, or the eigenvalue at the spectrum elbow (largest drop in log-λ), or the noise-floor eigenvalue estimated from a held-out/shuffled split. Don't leave `τ` as a free knob to fiddle.
- `a_{α,τ}`: scale-preservation constant, chosen so `tr(M_{α,τ} Σ_q) = tr(Σ_q)`. Mandatory — without it, stronger whitening changes reward variance and thus GRPO advantage scale independently of which directions matter, and a "result" could be altered optimization dynamics, not covariance equalization.

Initial sweep: `α ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75}`, `τ` fixed.
Whitened reward: `r_M = −(q(ĥ) − q(h))ᵀ M_{α,τ} (q(ĥ) − q(h)) = −eᵀ M e`.

⚠ Closed-form precondition (Rev 3 — the load-bearing addition). The marginal naming reward under `M` is `Δr ∝ eᵀ M Δ_c = Σ_i f(λ_i)(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)`, with `f(λ) = (λ+τ)^{−α}`. `M` reweights the per-eigendirection products `(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)`; it cannot manufacture a product that is zero. Therefore:
- Whitening raises naming reward for `c` only in eigendirections where both the residual `e` and `Δ_c` have support, and it helps only if that support sits in the low-`λ` directions whitening up-weights.
- If the AR's residual is ~0 in the directions `Δ_c` occupies (the redundancy case), `(eᵀu_i) ≈ 0` there and no `M`, at any `α`, creates naming reward. This is the modal failure mode the residual figure (Gate 0a) and the eigendirection-overlap figure (Gate 0b) test before training. It is not noise amplification (which `τ`/`α`/scale defend against) — it is the reward having no gradient to give, defendable by nothing.

⚠ Whitening the online AR may be the wrong sign — run the diagnostic as a sign test. Apply the same `M` to the online critic/AR reconstruction loss only if the diagnostic says it helps. The concern: whitening the AR pushes it to reconstruct `Δ_c` from contextual tokens better → shrinks `e` along `Δ_c` → reduces the AV's marginal incentive to name `c`. So:
- Diagnostic (run early, one knob, now a sign test): (a) whiten reward + online AR; (b) whiten reward only, AR standard. Rev-2 expected (a) ≥ (b). Rev-3 prediction: on target read-rate, (b) may beat (a). If (b) > (a) → AR whitening is counterproductive (it eats the residual the AV would otherwise be paid to name); run reward-only. If (a) > (b) → the standard-AR reward is incoherent in whitened space (AR can't reconstruct what the reward now scores) and AR whitening is needed despite the residual-shrink cost. Either outcome is informative; do not assume (a).
- Keep the existing AV-SFT and AR-SFT checkpoints unchanged for the first experiment — do not retrain AR-SFT with whitening by default.

Targeted up-weighting, and the unsupervised-vs-supervised honesty (Rev 3). Blanket `Σ_q^{−1}` is expected to over-amplify noise (and per the audit, the residual stream is ~90% rank, so the noise floor may be milder than Rev 2 assumed — recheck in Gate 0b, don't assume). The fix is to up-weight only the functional-but-low-variance subspace: `M = I + Σ_{i∈functional} (w_i−1) u_i u_iᵀ`. State which subspace you are betting on, because it determines whether the project is still unsupervised:
- (i) Span of your concept/CAA directions. Uses the labels to pick what to up-weight. This is "reweight the reward toward the probe direction" — barely-disguised supervision. If this is the bet, the honest baseline is a directly supervised reward term for naming the concept, and you must show whitening-within-the-subspace beats it. It is not obvious it does.
- (ii) CCA-against-the-model's-continuation (2604.20682's method) — directions predictive of the model's own continuation that reconstruction ignores. Genuinely unsupervised, but these are output-coupled directions, which are the ones already read — so (ii) likely does nothing for the unread targets. State this tension explicitly rather than implying (ii) saves the unsupervised framing for your targets.
- Plain `Σ_q^{−1}` is the upper-aggressiveness ablation, run to confirm the failure mode, not as the headline. `R_{c,k} = ‖P_k Δ_c‖²/‖Δ_c‖²` still guards against interpreting a null when the target wasn't in the up-weighted subspace.

Why "unsupervised" is the selling point (and the threat to it): `Σ_q` needs no labels — that's what separates this from a supervised Activation Oracle. But the version expected to work (subspace (i)) reintroduces labels through the subspace choice. The distinctive contribution survives only if an unsupervised-`Σ_q` reweighting (or subspace (ii)) moves the targets, or if subspace (i) demonstrably beats direct supervision. Decide and preregister which claim you are making.

Reward-geometry / optimization-dynamics confound. Stronger whitening changes reward/advantage scale. Defenses: targeted up-weighting, the `τ` floor, `α<1`, and scale-preservation `a_{α,τ}`, plus the per-arm logging in Gate 3. The honest possibility this raises: if the functional content is geometrically entangled with the noise floor (below resolvable spectrum in the operator that matters — see the Jacobian caveat), no reward-reweighting separates it → the gap needs a supervised signal. Gate 0b tests this before training.

Code location. In nanoNLA's RL trainer the reward is computed by normalizing `pred`/`gold` and taking MSE (`compute_rewards`); insert `M^{1/2}` into the squared-error term (`r_M = −‖M^{1/2}(q(ĥ)−q(h))‖²`). If using a TRL-`GRPOTrainer` loop (`kameshkanna/nla-train`), it's the `reward_funcs` callable. For the AR-whitening arm, apply `M^{1/2}` to the critic loss where the AR MSE is computed (in the released repo, the analogue of `loss.nla_critic_loss`).

## 4. Gate structure (run strictly in order; each gate can kill the project cheaply)
For each gate: hypothesis · expected-if-true · expected-under-boring-alternative · baseline/control · sanity check · failure modes · why now.

### Gate 0a — De-risk FIRST: residual alignment + nameability, on the baseline checkpoint (no training, no `Σ_q`, one afternoon)
This replaces "make the `Σ_q` spectrum plot" as the first action. It is strictly cheaper (no covariance estimation), strictly more decisive (closed-form tie to whether the intervention can do anything), and it routes around the model-matching hazard because it is a single within-model measurement.
- Hypothesis: H1 — residual alignment `⟨e, Δ_c⟩` predicts read-rate; and the "unread" label reflects a reward gap, not a judge/nameability artifact.
- Method. Run the baseline AV→AR round-trip (syvb λ=0 `base`, or the released Qwen2.5-7B / Gemma-3-27B NLA matched to your read-rates) on the corrigibility and refusal contrast pairs. For present-polarity activations, compute:
   1. Residual alignment `⟨q(h) − q(ĥ_base), v_c⟩`, `v_c = Δ_c/‖Δ_c‖`, for read concepts (refusal, truth_value) and unread (corrigibility, harmful_topic). (`ĥ_base` = AR value-head output at the last token of the baseline AV's explanation, normalized — `normalize_activation(pred, mse_scale)` in the released code.)
   2. Explanation-text decodability of the unread concept. Embed the baseline AV's explanations on corrigibility-present activations (sentence encoder, BoW logistic, or the AR's internal rep) and classify against the corrigibility label. Corrigibility is an abstract disposition an explanation can carry diffusely ("weighing whether to defer to the user's correction") without the judge crediting a named mention.
- Decision rule (this is the project's main fork):
   - Residual along unread targets ≈ 0 (≈ refusal-negative levels): redundancy/output-coupling is operative, whitening is geometrically blocked, stop before training. Publishable: "decodable-but-unread is a reconstruction-redundancy phenomenon; reward reweighting cannot fix it without supervision." (The audit's mixed evidence — high-rank activations vs low-rank Jacobian — means this is a genuinely open prior, not a foregone conclusion; the figure resolves it for ~half a day.)
   - Residual sizeable but the explanation text already encodes the concept (decodability ≫ chance while read-rate ~0): nameability/judge artifact, not a reward gap. The fix is a better read-rate metric or a naming-pressure term, not whitening. Re-scope.
   - Residual sizeable AND text does not encode the concept: the only branch where H1's residual form is live and whitening has something to amplify. Proceed to Gate 0b, with residual alignment as the headline predictor (static a/b/c demoted to secondary).
- ⚠ Model-matching (silent-bug warning). Residuals, `ĥ_base`, `Δ_c` must all come from the same model your read-rates are from (Qwen `Δ_c`+`ĥ_base` with Qwen read-rates; Gemma with Gemma). Do not mix syvb's Qwen3-8B with the Exp-2 Qwen2.5-7B/Gemma read-rates.
- Sanity check: a random unit direction must have residual alignment ≈ 0 and present-energy ≈ `d^{−1}`; if a random direction looks concept-like, the pipeline is broken (normalization, centering, model-matching). Refusal should show high residual alignment (it's output-coupled → AR can't reconstruct it from prose) — if refusal's residual is ~0, either the AR is unexpectedly good at it or the round-trip is mis-wired.
- ⚠ Power. ~7 concepts → a monotone claim is a smell test, not a verdict. Flat → real stop signal. Monotone → "keep going," decisive version needs the expanded panel (Gate 1). Don't over-conclude from 7 points.
- Failure modes: computing residuals from the wrong model; `ĥ_base` extracted at the wrong token (must be the AR's last-token value-head output, per the released `loss.py`); judge/nameability artifact mistaken for a reward gap (check 2 catches it); n=7 underpowered.
- Why now: half a day, existing checkpoints, closed-form tie to whether the intervention can work, and it is what both the reward-geometry derivation and the citation audit independently point at. No GPU, no `Σ_q`, before there's a residual to amplify.

### Gate 0b — `Σ_q` eigendirection-overlap (the corrected spectrum check; only in the residual-nonzero branch)
This is "figure two," and it replaces the bare `Σ_q`-spectrum noise-floor plot. Only run it if Gate 0a's third branch holds.
- Hypothesis: the residual–concept co-occupancy `(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)` sits in low-`λ` eigendirections that whitening up-weights — i.e., the intervention has a lever.
- Method. Estimate `Σ_q = Cov(q(h))` on the generic distribution (random web text through the model at L) — normalized space, not concept pairs. Eigendecompose. Plot, per concept, the product `(eᵀu_i)(Δ_cᵀu_i)` against `λ_i`. Also report the static predictors as secondary context: (a) generic projected variance `v_cᵀΣ_q v_c`; (b) present-activation energy fraction; (c) separability `Δ_cᵀ(Σ_q+τI)⁻¹Δ_c`; (d) CCA-against-continuation overlap.
- Expected if true: for unread-but-residual-bearing targets, the co-occupancy mass is in low-`λ` directions → whitening up-weights exactly those → lever exists. For read controls, residual is large but may sit in higher-`λ` directions (already named, no need to up-weight).
- ⚠ The Jacobian caveat (audit). The "is the functional content resolvable" question is, per 2605.14258, properly about the propagation Jacobian's active subspace, not `Σ_q`'s spectrum. If Gate 0b is ambiguous, the sharper (more expensive) version projects `e` and `Δ_c` onto the Jacobian's active subspace. Note this as a limitation; don't claim the `Σ_q` spectrum settles resolvability.
- Expected under boring alternative: co-occupancy is in high-`λ` directions or absent → whitening's up-weighting misses it → intervention has no lever even though residual is nonzero → stop or rethink the metric.
- Subtype robustness (cheap, do here): for umbrella labels (corrigibility, sycophancy), recompute residual alignment + co-occupancy for the umbrella and 2–3 subtypes (corrigibility → accept-correction / goal-modification / shutdown-deference; sycophancy → false-agreement / excessive-praise / truthful-agreement). Stable → umbrella fine. Divergent → heterogeneous label shrinking `Δ_c`; split before trusting.
- Why now: tells you how to reweight (which subspace) and whether reweighting can reach the residual — the precondition for any Gate-3 effect. Cheap (one `Σ_q` estimate + projections of data in hand).

### Gate 1 — Benchmark on the training model + free substrate pre-check
- Hypothesis: the decodable-but-unread phenomenon exists on the model you'll train (Qwen3-8B L24, or 7B — see §6).
- Free pre-check (no training): run your concept-pair activations (target layer) through syvb's `base` AV; judge-score read-rate. If corrigibility reads fine there → gap is model-specific → fall back to Qwen2.5-7B L20 where Exp-2 established it. If unread → proceed.
- Method: panel = targets (corrigibility, harmful_topic, + 3–4 more decodable-but-unread: false-agreement, deception/withholding, sandbagging-disposition, a knows-but-doesn't-say factual concept) and read-controls (refusal, truth_value, neg_sentiment). For each: probe-decodable (AUROC ≫ chance) and BoW-clean for targets. Read-rate = judge-scored "names the concept" on present-polarity real activations (validate the judge on obvious ± first).
- Matched controls (make matching variables explicit): probe AUROC, class prevalence, BoW leakage, prompt length & token position, next-token/output-coupling effect, average activation norm before normalization, baseline explanation length.
- ⚠ Probe-leakage transfer check (Rev 3): AUROC 0.97 / BoW 0.50 rules out lexical leakage but not stylistic/source/syntactic features. Test whether the probe direction transfers to held-out naturalistic activations where the disposition arises without paired-prompt scaffolding. If it doesn't transfer, "decodable" is partly construction leakage and "unread" may be correct — re-pick the target.
- Sanity check: length-residualize the probe; shuffled-label ≈ 0.5; pair-level CV.
- Failure modes: judge conflates "names concept" with "affirmation language"; concept pairs differ in style/source not just the concept; probe exploits construction leakage (transfer check catches). Hand-check judge calls.
- Why now: without this panel + benchmark, "promising" has nothing to attach to; one concept is an anecdote. The panel is the unit of analysis.

### Gate 2 — Baseline (frugal: reuse syvb)
- Hypothesis: a standard-reward NLA on this model reads the controls and misses the targets.
- Method (preferred): don't train — score syvb's λ=0 `base` AV+AR on the Gate-1 benchmark; verify one FVE number against syvb's `results`. Fallback: train your own, standard reward, LoRA on unquantized base, vLLM trainer, `--train-critic`.
- Expected if true: controls read, targets ~0 (same pattern as the released NLAs). This is your control arm.
- Expected under boring alternative: baseline already reads the targets → no gap on this model → stop/retarget.
- ⚠ FVE-headroom check (Rev 3): confirm syvb base FVE is high enough that target read-rate isn't already in the metric's noise floor. If FVE ≈ 0.3 and read-rates of subtle concepts are erratic across seeds at baseline, every Gate-3 delta will be ambiguous — consider the 7B/released checkpoints (0.6–0.8 FVE regime) for the headline instead.
- Sanity check: grep generated text for CJK (loud nanoNLA smoke test for a dead injection hook); FVE in a sane range; legible explanations.
- Failure modes: loaded the wrong/penalized syvb checkpoint (verify λ=0); FVE so low any Gate-3 null is uninterpretable.
- Why now: can't interpret the modified arm without a same-pipeline baseline; reuse means it's free.

### Gate 3 — Modified NLA (the experiment)
- Hypothesis: H2 — whitening raises target read-rate at tolerable ordinary-FVE cost, conditional on Gate 0b showing a lever.
- Method: continue RL from syvb's `base` (`--resume-from-lora`) with the whitened reward (§3). Run the reward-only vs reward+AR diagnostic as a sign test (§3) at one `α` first — it determines whether you whiten the AR at all. Then sweep `α ∈ {0,0.25,0.5,0.75}`, `τ` fixed, scale-preserved. On 2× H100 runs are sequential → baseline + 2 settings (mild, aggressive) first, dense sweep + ≥2 seeds only if promising. Calibrate wall-clock with a 20-step run.
- Per-arm logging (mandatory, rules out optimization-dynamics confound): mean reward; within-GRPO-group reward std; critic loss under standard metric; critic loss under `M`; response length; extraction-failure rate. Keep the response cap fixed across all arms (the release notes warn the critic rewards verbosity unless length is bounded).
- Expected if true: as `α` rises, target read-rate rises (corrigibility 0 → positive) selectively (`eᵀMΔ_c/‖e‖‖Δ_c‖` up more for targets than controls), ordinary FVE degrades gracefully then knees; a region exists with target gains + read-controls preserved + acceptable FVE, and the gains pass Gate-3b.
- Expected under boring alternative: target read-rate flat at every `α` until FVE collapses → whitening up-weighted noise or there was no residual to amplify (Gate 0a/0b should have caught this — if they didn't, revisit them); or gains are confabulation (fail Gate-3b); or AR whitening ate the residual (the sign test detects). A flat-then-collapse null is less ambiguous than in Rev 2 because Gate 0a/0b pre-screened the residual — if you reached Gate 3, a null more likely means "lever existed but RL couldn't exploit it / co-training issue" than "wrong mechanism." Say which.
- Baseline/control: syvb base. Report deltas. Spectrum-preserving shuffled-Σ control: same eigenvalue spectrum, eigenvectors replaced by a random orthogonal basis, same normalization constant — distinguishes "using the model's actual geometry" from "upweighting arbitrary directions." Plus the mild-whitening continuity check (α=0.25 ≈ baseline in FVE, length, read-controls).
- Failure modes: noise amplification → FVE collapse (τ/α/scale defend); AR whitening shrinks the residual (sign test); whitening shifts which high-variance content is named rather than adding low-variance content (check controls + read explanations); confabulation (Gate-3b).
- Why now: the only step that produces a new result; everything else exists to make it interpretable — and Gate 0a/0b exist to ensure it isn't run when geometrically blocked.

### Gate 3b — Faithfulness battery (required for any read-rate gain; REORDERED in Rev 3)
A read-rate gain counts only if it survives this. The judge measures "names the concept," not "names it because it's there." Point 4 is now primary — it is the only check grounded in external truth and is the one the others reduce to.
1. (PRIMARY) Probe consistency against the target model's labels. Conditional on an explanation naming the concept, is the actual `Δ_c` projection / probe score on the target model's activation higher than on matched examples that don't name it? If not, the naming is decorative. This makes the probe's trustworthiness load-bearing (hence the Gate-1 transfer check). This is the success criterion, not the fourth item.
2. Context-hallucination audit. Hand-check whether modified explanations invent prompt facts more often than baseline. A gain that mainly raises confident false behavioral narratives is not an interpretability win.
3. Paraphrase / reorder / translation invariance. Meaning-preserving transforms (the NLA report's anti-steganography check) — gains must survive in the modified arm, not just baseline.
4. (WEAK under whitening — interpret with care) Phrase-neutralization + weighted-reconstruction change. Delete/replace only the target-concept phrase; measure change in weighted reconstruction error and in the reconstructed activation's projection onto `Δ_c`. Caveat (Rev 3): under whitening you optimized for `Δ_c`-aligned mentions, so deleting one will hurt weighted reconstruction by construction — that measures "did it learn the new reward," not faithfulness. The informative part is whether removal moves the `Δ_c` projection of the reconstructed activation; a confabulated mention won't, a grounded one will. Do not treat the weighted-reconstruction drop alone as faithfulness evidence.

## 5. Pre-registered success criteria
Decide before looking at Gate-3 numbers. Report two fidelity metrics but only ordinary FVE is the cost:
- `FVE_std` — ordinary directional FVE (baseline-comparable) = the cost budget.
- `FVE_M` — FVE under the whitened metric = report it, but it mostly shows the model optimized the new objective, so it is not a success criterion.

Gating precondition (Rev 3): Gate 0a must show nonzero residual along the targets and Gate 0b must show low-`λ` co-occupancy. If either fails, the project stops there with a negative finding; Gate-3 criteria below do not apply.
- Scale-it ("promising"): target read-rate rises ≥ 0.25 absolute on ≥ 2 target concepts, the marginal-reward ratio `eᵀMΔ_c/‖e‖‖Δ_c‖` rises more for targets than controls, AND read-control read-rates drop ≤ 0.10, AND `FVE_std` drops ≤ 0.08 (or stays above warm-start), AND the gains pass Gate-3b primary (probe consistency), AND the spectrum-preserving control does not reproduce the gain. Across ≥ 2 seeds. (Note the headroom risk: ≤0.08 FVE_std on a 0.3 base is ~27% relative; if the frugal baseline is this low, prefer the 0.6–0.8 FVE checkpoints so the budget is meaningful.)
- Pivot: gains close only past the `FVE_std` knee → mechanism plausibly right, intervention too costly → try a different `τ`/`α`, the reward-only vs reward+AR arm per the sign test, or reframe as "fix exists, trades FVE."
- Stop / rethink: no read-rate movement at any `α` before FVE collapse, and the spectrum-preserving control behaves the same → whitening isn't the lever (and Gate 0a/0b should have predicted this). Report the negative.

Hard rule: "the explanations look more interpretable" is not a signal — a standard NLA already produces reasonable text, and a confabulated whitening gain also looks reasonable. The signal is the baseline-vs-modified read-rate delta that passes Gate-3b's probe-consistency check, or it is nothing.

## 6. Concrete pipeline & compute (frugal path)
Trains exactly one thing — the whitened-reward RL fine-tune — and only if Gate 0a/0b clear.
1. Gate 0a (FIRST) — residual-alignment + nameability on the baseline checkpoint. Half a day, no `Σ_q`, no training. This is the figure that decides the project.
2. Gate 0b — only in the residual-nonzero branch: estimate `Σ_q` (normalized space, generic distribution), eigendecompose, plot residual–concept co-occupancy vs `λ`. Hours, no training.
3. Free substrate pre-check (Gate 1) — extract target-layer activations for your concept pairs; run through syvb's `base` AV; confirm the gap + probe transfer. No training.
4. Save `M_{α,τ}^{1/2}` per `α` with scale constants `a_{α,τ}`.
5. Baseline (Gate 2) — reuse syvb λ=0 `base`; verify one FVE number; check FVE headroom. No training unless unusable.
6. RL modified (Gate 3) — `train_rl_vllm.py`, `--resume-from-lora` onto syvb base, whitened reward; run the reward-only vs reward+AR sign test first, then sweep; vLLM rollout one card / trainable other, `--train-critic`, full per-arm logging, fixed response cap. Baseline + 2 knobs first; dense sweep + ≥2 seeds if promising.
7. Eval — judge read-rate + Gate-3b (probe consistency primary) + `FVE_std`/`FVE_M`, run identically against syvb base and every modified checkpoint.

Smoke-test with `scripts/smoke_fixed_pipeline.sh`. Calibrate wall-clock with 20 steps before committing — the sweep is sequential on 2 cards.

Model choice — Qwen2.5-7B L20 vs Qwen3-8B L24.
- 7B (the "old" model): Exp-2 corrigibility benchmark, baselines, concept pairs already exist → Gate 0a/0b/1 nearly free; released checkpoint (`Kameshr/nla-qwen2.5-7b-L20-av`, FVE ~0.28) + a clean TRL-GRPO repo (`kameshkanna/nla-train`) where the reward swap is one function. Downside: weak baseline ⇒ thin FVE headroom (the binding constraint, §7). The "too small/old" objection does not apply — that was about eval-awareness (needs scale); this runs on corrigibility, already proven represented on the 7B.
- Qwen3-8B L24 (newer): syvb base saves baseline training + free pre-check; more current. Downside: rebuild the benchmark, confirm the gap; same ~0.3 FVE headroom concern.
- Recommended: run Gate 0a on whichever you already have read-rates for (cheapest to know), since the residual figure is the same cost either way and decides everything. If a residual exists and whitening moves read-rate, port to the highest-FVE checkpoint available for the headline so the cost budget is meaningful — that may be a released 0.6–0.8 NLA rather than either frugal option.

## 7. Risks & honest failure modes
- No residual to amplify (the Rev-3 primary risk). If the AR already reconstructs the target from context, `⟨e, Δ_c⟩ ≈ 0` and whitening is null by construction at any strength. Gate 0a tests this for half a day before any GPU. This is the modal way the project ends, and ending it cheaply here is a good outcome.
- AR whitening pushes read-rate the wrong way. Whitening the AR shrinks the residual the AV would be paid to name. The §3 sign test detects it; default to reward-only unless the test says otherwise.
- Unsupervised framing collapses into supervision. The subspace-(i) version that's expected to work uses labels; benchmark it against a direct supervised naming reward or the selling point is gone. State which claim you're making (§3).
- Covariance space (was the Rev-1 bug). Normalized-space, generic-distribution. Estimating on contrast pairs inverts the mechanism. Sanity: refusal high-residual, random ≈ `d^{−1}`.
- FVE headroom. A ~0.3-FVE frugal baseline may put subtle-concept read-rate in the metric's noise floor; a ≤0.08 FVE_std budget is ~27% relative there. Prefer a 0.6–0.8 checkpoint for the headline.
- Confabulation (the worst outcome). Whitening could "succeed" by emitting false target mentions. Gate-3b probe-consistency (now primary) is mandatory.
- Optimization-dynamics confound. Scale-preservation `a_{α,τ}` + per-arm logging rule it out.
- Probe leakage. AUROC 0.97 / BoW 0.50 doesn't exclude stylistic/source/syntactic leakage; the Gate-1 transfer-to-naturalistic check guards it. If the probe doesn't transfer, "unread" may be correct.
- Citation scope (audit). 2604.20682 is GPT-2/Mistral on WikiText and warns against non-uniform reweighting; 2508.16929 says the residual stream is high-rank (not a noise tail); 2605.14258 is about the Jacobian, not `Σ_q`. None establishes that your concepts go unread for the residual reason — only Gate 0a does. Verify page-quotes before any submission.
- Co-training deadlock / critic non-adaptation. A Gate-3 null is narrowed (not eliminated) by the Gate-0 pre-screen + the sign test. Continuing from syvb base (rich AV) helps.
- syvb-baseline provenance. Tail-driven ~0.3 FVE, some hard failures; reproduce one `results` number; use λ=0.
- Heterogeneous umbrella labels. The Gate-0b subtype-stability check catches a label shrinking `Δ_c`.
- Eval validity. Single judge, finite panel; hand-check; per-concept reporting; treat sub-0.02 FVE deltas as noise (no sign claims without seeds).
- Scope creep into attribution/circuits. This intervenes on the reward and reads the explanation; it does not open the AV's internal circuit. Keep it there.

## 9. Key references (abstracts verified via search; confirm exact page-quotes against the PDF before citing)
- Variance Is Not Importance: Structural Analysis of Transformer Compressibility Across Model Scales — arXiv 2604.20682 (Salfati). GPT-2 124M + Mistral 7B, WikiText-2, >40 experiments. High-variance directions ~96% uncorrelated with predictive directions (CCA); variance-based redundancy tools "may systematically misidentify which dimensions are functionally important." Supports the premise (variance ≠ importance) AND warns against the fix: uniform quantization avoids the reconstruction wall, while "more sophisticated approaches that attempt to exploit structural redundancy consistently fall into the reconstruction wall." Borrow its CCA-against-continuation method to identify a functional subspace; cite the warning honestly. Not an NLA, not behavioral concepts.
- Dimensional Collapse in Transformer Attention Outputs: A Challenge for Sparse Dictionary Learning — arXiv 2508.16929 (Wang, Ge, Shu, He, Qiu; earlier-version title "Attention Layers Add Into Low-Dimensional Residual Subspaces"). Attention outputs ~60% effective rank; residual streams and MLP outputs ~90% (near-full-rank), across families/datasets; low rank shaped by the attention output-projection matrix; linked to SAE dead features. Use for "attention writes a low-dim subspace." Does NOT support "the residual tail is mostly noise" — the residual stream (what the NLA reads, what `Σ_q` is computed on) is the high-rank object.
- Dynamics of the Transformer Residual Stream: Coupling Spectral Geometry to Network Topology — arXiv 2605.14258 (Fernando & Guitchounts). Full Jacobian eigendecomposition across three production LLMs; monotonic spectral gradient through depth + cumulative low-rank bottleneck funnelling perturbations into few effective dimensions. This is the propagation operator, not `Σ_q` — to use it for resolvability you must project onto the Jacobian's active subspace, not the covariance spectrum. Does not justify a `Σ_q`-spectrum noise floor.
- Concepts Whisper While Syntax Shouts — arXiv 2605.01609. Unverified, LLM-survey-sourced; weakest analogue (unembedding space, lexical concepts, base models). Read before citing; supporting only.
- Steering Llama-2 with Contrastive Activation Additions (CAA) — Rimsky et al. For Gate-3b causal vectors; verify with magnitude-sweep + subtype stability, not just "it steers." Reports nothing about spectral location.
- Sycophancy Is Not One Thing: Causal Separation of Sycophantic Behaviors (OpenReview d24zTCznJu) and/or Sycophancy Hides Linearly in the Attention Heads (arXiv 2601.16644) — unverified, LLM-survey-sourced. If they hold, they motivate the subtype check (a single "sycophancy" vector is a blend). Verify before citing.
- Natural Language Autoencoders — Fraser-Taliente, Kantamneni, Ong et al., transformer-circuits.pub/2026/nla. The method; its eval-awareness + hidden-motivation positives falsify "meta ⇒ unread." Released code confirms the reward is direction-only (`2(1−cos)`), the AR is a truncated backbone + `Linear(d,d)` head read at the last token, and the online AR is the live reward model — the basis for the residual-reward derivation in §0/§2.
- Jacob Steinhardt — Research as a Stochastic Decision Process. The de-risking philosophy these gates implement.

Load-bearing summary for the audit: only 2604.20682 (mechanism) and the two low-rank papers do real work, and the audit re-scoped all three — 2604.20682 half-warns against the fix, 2508.16929 says the residual stream is high-rank (undercutting the noise-floor justification), and 2605.14258 measures the Jacobian, not `Σ_q`. None settles whether your concepts go unread for the residual reason; Gate 0a does. The sycophancy citations and Concepts Whisper are unverified survey snippets — confirm arXiv IDs and claims before any of them goes near a writeup (this is exactly where fabricated citations hide).

## What this design deliberately does not claim
- It does not assume meta-level/behavioral concepts are low-variance (the NLA paper's eval-awareness result falsifies that prior); H1 is a measured hypothesis about specific observed omissions, tested via residual alignment in Gate 0a.
- It does not equate reward-relevance with variance/energy; the operative quantity is the reconstruction residual `⟨e, Δ_c⟩`, and the static predictors are confounded proxies.
- It does not assume the intervention can work; whitening is geometrically blocked unless the residual co-occupies low-`λ` eigendirections with `Δ_c` (Gate 0b precondition).
- It does not assume whitening the AR helps; it may shrink the residual and reduce read-rate (the §3 sign test decides).
- It does not assume the unsupervised framing survives; if the working intervention uses label-defined subspaces, the honest baseline is direct supervision.
- It does not treat higher FVE (or `FVE_M`) as the goal; ordinary `FVE_std` is a bounded cost.
- It does not count a read-rate gain as success unless it passes the faithfulness battery, probe-consistency primary (Gate-3b).
- A faithful-but-small reproduction is not a result; the result is the gap between two NLAs differing only in reward geometry, on a panel, that survives faithfulness — or a clean negative from Gate 0a/0b (redundancy / no lever / nameability artifact), which is itself publishable.
